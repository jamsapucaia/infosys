Q36. Explain clearly: chart of accounts, debit/credit, general ledger, balance sheet, income statement.

  • Chart of accounts: Is the list of all GL accounts used by a company.
  • Debit/credit:
  • General Ledger: The GL contains summary-level data for every asset, liability, equity, revenue, and expense account of an organization.

  • Income statement: Report that shows the company’s revenues and expenses during a particular period in time.

Q37. What is the difference between installing and initializing a purchased accounting software package?

  • Installing: Copying the software to the computer’s hard disk and getting it ready to run.
  • Initializing: Entering specifics on the Company into the system so as to make it ready for day-to day use.

Q38. What main steps are involved in initializing an accounting system?

  • Entering particulars on the Company (e.g. name address)
  • identifying each account (card) by name and by code number for all the Company’s assets, liabilities, equities, revenues, and expenses - establishing the Chart of Accounts.
